Research

Heat, interfaces, and phase change across scales

The HiPhy Lab studies heat transport, fluid flow, and interfacial phase-change phenomena in systems where temperature, pressure, and material interfaces strongly influence transport processes.

CO₂ sublimation and desublimation

We investigate how carbon dioxide changes phase between solid and vapour, with emphasis on interfacial transport, morphology evolution, and thermal resistance.

Cryogenic carbon capture

We study low-temperature approaches for separating CO₂ from gas mixtures by controlled desublimation and subsequent phase-change pathways.

Supercritical CO₂ heat transfer

We examine heat transfer in supercritical CO₂ flows, including pseudo-boiling, heat-transfer deterioration, mixed convection, and flow instabilities.

Fusion-relevant thermal management

We explore advanced cooling concepts for high-heat-flux systems, including the potential use of supercritical CO₂ in fusion-relevant thermal environments.

Thermal energy storage

We study heat and mass transport in thermal and thermochemical energy storage systems, with attention to material behaviour and reactor-scale performance.

Diagnostics and modelling

Our work combines visualization, optical diagnostics, thermal measurements, theory, and numerical modelling to understand transport processes near interfaces.
