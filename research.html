HiPhy Lab · Research

Heat, interfaces, and phase change across scales

We study heat transport, fluid flow, and interfacial phase-change phenomena in systems where temperature, pressure, material interfaces, and flow strongly influence thermal transport.

Currently

Current interests include CO₂ sublimation, CO₂ desublimation, phase change at supercritical pressures, and transport in porous media.

Research themes

What we study

CO₂ sublimation

How does solid CO₂ disappear into the surrounding gas?

We study the sublimation of solid carbon dioxide, where heat and mass transfer are coupled at a moving solid-vapour interface. This includes sublimation of dry ice in controlled environments, interfacial temperature dynamics, vapour-density gradients, and the role of ambient pressure and gas composition.

Dry ice Sublimation Moving interfaces Vapour transport

CO₂ desublimation

How does CO₂ deposit as a solid on cold surfaces?

We investigate CO₂ desublimation from gas mixtures, with emphasis on solid-layer growth, frost morphology, thermal resistance, and transport through the boundary layer near cold surfaces. This work supports the development of cryogenic carbon capture technologies.

Desublimation Cryogenic carbon capture Frost growth Heat exchangers

Phase change at supercritical pressures

What happens to heat transfer near pseudo-critical conditions?

At supercritical pressures, fluids do not undergo classical liquid-vapour phase change, but their properties can vary sharply near the pseudo-critical region. We study heat transfer, pseudo-boiling-like behaviour, heat-transfer enhancement and deterioration, and flow instabilities in supercritical-pressure systems.

Supercritical CO₂ Pseudo-boiling Heat-transfer deterioration Thermal management

Transport in porous media

How do heat and mass move through complex porous structures?

Many phase-change and energy-storage systems involve porous, granular, frost-like, or composite materials. We study heat and mass transport in such media, including effective thermal resistance, diffusion, permeability, morphology evolution, and reactor-scale transport limits.

Porous media Heat and mass transfer Thermal resistance Energy storage
