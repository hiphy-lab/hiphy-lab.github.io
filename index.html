Understanding heat transport and phase transitions at interfaces

The HiPhy Lab at the University of Twente studies heat transport, fluid flow, and interfacial phase-change phenomena across a wide range of temperatures and pressures.

Our research focuses on heat transfer and fluid flow involving phase changes, with a particular emphasis on CO₂, investigated through both experiments and simulations. Understanding these transitions is key to advancing energy-efficient technologies that use CO₂ as a natural working fluid - from cryogenic carbon capture and ultra-low-temperature refrigeration to high-temperature heat pumps, spray cooling, cold-chain logistics, thermal energy storage, and thermal management in electronic devices.

Research

Research themes

CO₂ Phase Change

Sublimation, desublimation, melting, and interfacial transport processes in carbon dioxide systems.

Supercritical CO₂

Heat transfer, pseudo-boiling, flow instabilities, and thermal management using supercritical CO₂.

Cryogenic Carbon Capture

Low-temperature CO₂ separation through controlled desublimation and phase-transition pathways.

Thermal Energy Storage

Heat and mass transport in thermal and thermochemical energy storage systems.
