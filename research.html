HiPhy Lab · Research

Heat, interfaces, and phase change across scales

We study heat transport, fluid flow, and interfacial phase-change phenomena in systems where temperature, pressure, material interfaces, and flow strongly influence thermal transport.

Currently

Current interests include CO₂ sublimation and desublimation, supercritical CO₂ heat transfer, cryogenic carbon capture, fusion-relevant thermal management, and heat and mass transport in thermal energy storage systems.

Research themes

What we study

CO₂ phase change

How does CO₂ grow, melt, sublime, and desublime?

Carbon dioxide can change phase through solid, vapour, liquid, and supercritical states depending on temperature and pressure. We investigate CO₂ sublimation and desublimation with emphasis on moving interfaces, morphology evolution, interfacial transport, and thermal resistance.

CO₂ Sublimation Desublimation Moving interfaces

Cryogenic carbon capture

Can we capture CO₂ by freezing it out of gas mixtures?

Cryogenic carbon capture separates CO₂ from gas mixtures by cooling the flow until CO₂ deposits as a solid. We study how solid CO₂ layers grow, how they affect heat transfer, and how pressure, gas composition, flow, and surface temperature influence the capture process.

Carbon capture Cryogenics Frost growth Heat exchangers

Supercritical fluids

What controls heat transfer near the pseudo-critical region?

Supercritical CO₂ is attractive for compact heat exchangers, high-temperature heat pumps, and advanced thermal cycles. Near the pseudo-critical region, fluid properties vary strongly, and heat transfer can be enhanced, deteriorated, or destabilized.

Supercritical CO₂ Pseudo-boiling Mixed convection Thermal management

High-heat-flux systems

How can we cool extreme thermal environments?

Future energy systems, including fusion-relevant components, require cooling strategies for very high heat fluxes. We explore thermal-management concepts where coolant choice, channel geometry, material constraints, and stability limits are tightly coupled.

Fusion cooling High heat flux Minichannels Thermal design

Thermal storage

How do heat and mass transport limit energy storage materials?

Thermal and thermochemical energy storage materials store heat through phase change or reversible chemical reactions. Their performance depends strongly on transport through porous, granular, encapsulated, or composite structures.

Thermal storage Thermochemical materials Heat and mass transfer Reactors

Diagnostics and modelling

How can we see and model transport near moving interfaces?

We combine visualization, optical diagnostics, heat-flux and temperature measurements, theory, and numerical modelling to understand transport processes near interfaces.

Schlieren Back-light imaging Heat flux Modelling
Current directions

Ongoing research directions

CO₂ desublimation for cryogenic carbon capture

Understanding how solid CO₂ layers form on cold surfaces in gas mixtures, and how morphology and boundary-layer transport affect capture performance.

Supercritical CO₂ cooling in confined channels

Investigating heat-transfer enhancement and deterioration in supercritical CO₂ flows relevant to compact heat exchangers and high-heat-flux cooling.

Diagnostics for interfacial phase change

Developing optical and thermal methods to resolve transport near moving phase-change interfaces.

Heat and mass transport in energy storage systems

Studying transport limitations in thermal and thermochemical storage materials, composites, and reactor concepts.
